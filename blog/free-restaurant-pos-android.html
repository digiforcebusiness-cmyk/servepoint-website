Best Free Restaurant POS for Android in 2026

"Free POS app" is one of the most searched terms by new restaurant owners — and one of the most misleading. There are genuinely free options that work well for very small operations. And there are "free" apps that will cost you significantly once you need anything beyond the most basic features. Here's the honest breakdown.

What "free" actually means in POS apps

Most free POS apps operate on a freemium model: the core app is free, but critical features — multi-device support, kitchen display, inventory management, detailed reports — sit behind a paid tier. The app is free the same way a car with no engine is free.

The other common free model is transaction-based pricing: the app itself costs nothing, but the payment processor charges 2–3% of every transaction. For a restaurant doing €5,000/month in sales, that's €100–150/month in processing fees alone — far more than a flat monthly subscription.

When a free POS is actually enough

A free POS works well if: you're a single operator with no separate kitchen, your menu has fewer than 30 items, you don't need multi-device sync, you're just getting started and testing viability, and you process cash payments only (avoiding per-transaction fees).

In this scenario, a basic free app is fine. You're not losing much, and you're not paying anything during a phase where every cost matters.

When to upgrade to a paid plan

As soon as any of these apply, a paid plan starts making financial sense: you hire a second person (needing multi-device), you add a kitchen screen, you want inventory auto-tracking, you need reliable customer support, or you're processing enough volume that per-transaction fees exceed a flat subscription.
